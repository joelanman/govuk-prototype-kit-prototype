Act as a certificate provider

You may be asked to be a certificate provider to help someone (the donor) when they’re making their lasting power of attorney (LPA).

Providing a certificate for an LPA confirms that in your opinion:

  • the donor understands the decisions they’ve made in the LPA and its purpose
  • the donor has not been put under pressure to make it
  • there is no fraud involved in its creation
  • there is no other reason it cannot be created

As a certificate provider you would need to:

  • review the LPA
  • witness the LPA
  • provide a certificate
  • prove your identity

Discuss the LPA with the donor

You’ll need to read the LPA and talk to the donor to check they understand it. You should discuss the LPA in private, without attorneys or other people present.

You should consider if they can:

  • understand how the LPA works
  • retain that information long enough to be able to make a decision
  • weigh up the information available to make the decision
  • communicate their decision, for example by talking, using sign language or movements like squeezing a hand

If they seem unusually anxious or are acting differently to how they normally would, consider if they’re being pressured into making the LPA.

If you have concerns about the LPA registration

If you do not feel you can certify the LPA, do not sign it and contact the Office of the Public Guardian (OPG) instead.

You can also object to the LPA which could prevent it from being registered.

If you do not have concerns about the LPA registration

Sign the LPA as a witness

If you’re confident the donor understands the LPA you’ll need to watch the donor sign the LPA. You must confirm you’ve witnessed this by signing it yourself, either online or on paper.

Prove your identity and sign to certify the LPA

You’ll need to prove your identity before you can provide a certificate.

If you fail the identity check, the donor will have to make a new LPA.

If you do not want to be a certificate provider

If you do not want to be someone's certificate provider you can refuse the role by telling the donor to contact OPG to start the LPA process again.

How to provide a certificate for the LPA

Read the LPA and talk to the donor about it. Witness the donor's signature.

OPG will contact you to certify the LPA separately. You can do it online or by post.

If your personal information is incorrect in the LPA, you’ll need to contact OPG which could prevent it from being registered.

You'll also need to prove your identity as part of the process.

Providing a certificate online

You’ll need documents or information to prove your identity using GOV.UK One Login.You'll also need the reference number from your certificate provider email or letter.

If you’ve not used GOV.UK One Login before, you’ll be asked to set up an account.

Start now

Providing a certificate by post

If you've received a letter from OPG about being a certificate provider, it should include the paper form. If not, contact OPG and ask for a paper form to be sent to you.

After you’ve certified the LPA

OPG will let the donor know when it has received your certification and has confirmed your identity.

Your role in the process is complete.
