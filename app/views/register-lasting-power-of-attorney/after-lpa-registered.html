After the lasting power of attorney is registered

The LPA is only registered when:

  • it's signed by the donor, attorneys and certificate provider
  • the donor and certificate provider have confirmed their identity
  • the 4 week waiting period is over

Once this is done, the Office of the Public Guardian (OPG) will notify:

  • the donors
  • the attorneys
  • any replacement attorneys

Anyone the donor has chosen to receive updates on their behalf, known as a ‘correspondent’, will get a letter confirming the LPA is registered.

Anyone the donor has asked to be told about their LPA, known as ‘people to notify’, will only get notified about the intention to register. They won’t be told when the LPA is registered.

When attorneys can use the LPA

The attorneys can only use the LPA when it’s been registered.

Personal welfare LPAs can only be used when the donor no longer has the mental capacity to make their own decisions.

Property and affairs LPAs can be used as soon as they are registered, with the donor's permission, or just when the donor is unable to make their own decisions, depending on what the donor has chosen in the LPA

This could be on a short-term basis, for example when the donor’s on holiday, or to provide long-term help.

When making decisions on behalf of the donor, the attorney needs to prove they’re named on the LPA.

Find out more about how attorneys use the LPA.

Changes you need to report

The attorney needs to report changes to OPG such as:

  • a change of address
  • a change of name
  • an attorney resigns ('disclaims')
  • an attorney dies
  • an attorney loses mental capacity
  • an attorney becomes bankrupt
  • an attorney divorces the donor

Replacement attorneys can also report changes to OPG, so they can step in if needed.

As long as the donor still has mental capacity, they can also report changes.

When the LPA ends

There are several reasons an LPA will end including if:

  • the donor dies
  • the donor cancels the LPA
  • an attorney cannot act and there's no replacement attorney
