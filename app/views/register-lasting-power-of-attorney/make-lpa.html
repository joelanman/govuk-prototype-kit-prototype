Make your lasting power of attorney

While preparing to make your LPA you should decide:

  • the type of LPA you want
  • who you want as your attorneys
  • if you want any replacement attorneys
  • how you want your attorneys to make decisions
  • if you want to give any restrictions and conditions
  • who'll be your certificate provider
  • if you want to notify someone about the LPA

You can ask someone else to help you create the LPA, for example a family member, friend or solicitor but the decisions you make must be your own.

This guidance is about LPAs that will be used in England or Wales. Read about power of attorney in Scotland and power of attorney in Northern Ireland.

You do not need to live in the UK or be a British citizen. Your LPA will be legally binding only in England and Wales. For example, if you’re making a property and affairs LPA, it can only be used for your properties and assets in England and Wales.

What you'll need

What you'll need

You'll need:

  • documents or information to prove your identity
  • a debit or credit card to pay the application fee
  • the name and address of your certificate provider
  • the name and address of each person to be notified

For each attorney and replacement attorney, you'll need their:

  • full name
  • address
  • date of birth
  • telephone number, if this is a mobile number they'll receive text updates
  • email address (if they have one)

If you nominate someone to receive updates and reminders on your behalf, you’ll have to provide their:

  • name
  • address
  • date of birth
  • telephone number, if this is a mobile number they'll receive text updates
  • email address (if they have one)

If you’re eligible for a reduced fee for your LPA, you must also have the evidence to support your application.

Start making your LPA

You can make an LPA online or by post. Your certificate provider and attorneys can use a different method to you.

Make your LPA online

You'll need documents or information to prove your identify using GOV.UK One Login.

If you’ve not used GOV.UK One Login before, you’ll be asked to set up an account.

You can save and return to your application at a later date if you need to.

Start now

Make an LPA by post

You'll need to contact the Office of the Public Guardian (OPG) to request an application pack by post. This pack will include the forms and instructions.

After you've signed and paid for the LPA

Your certificate provider and attorneys will be contacted using the details you provide. They’ll be asked to check the details of your LPA.
